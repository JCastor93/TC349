Part 1

Project Instructions

Adding Fish

To add a fish:

  1. Click on the "Add Fish" menu
  2. Choose a fish from the menu.

Removing Fish

  1. Click on the "File" menu
  2. Select "Trash Can"
  3. Drag the fish you wish to remove over the trashcan with the mouse

Adding Decor

  1. Click on the "Add Decor" menu
  2. Choose a decor item from the menu.

Saving The Aquarium Items

  1. Click on the "File" menu
  2. Select "Save As"
  3. Enter the desired file name and click "OK" to save the aquarium

Loading a Saved Aquarium

  1. Click on the "File" menu
  2. Select "Open"
  3. Locate the desired aquarium file and click "OK" to open the saved aquarium

Counting Beta Fish

  1. Click on the "File" menu
  2. Select "Count Beta Fish"
  3. Click "OK" to close the dialog box

Interface Decisions

Part 2Ideas

We intend to implement a game mechanic based upon the cleanliness of the tank, where factors in the environment will contribute to the rate at which the tank becomes dirty. The user's goal will be to maintain balance in the tank, as the rate at which random harmful effects accrue increases, making the game more difficult over time.

Part 2

Game Instructions

When the program starts, the game will begin. The game has five levels, each lasting roughly a minute. The algae will appear more frequently and fewer fish will automatically be generated with each level you pass. The object of the game is to feed the fish before they starve, as well as to remove any algae that appears in the fish tank. When there are more than fifteen algae in the tank, or the fish are not fed in time, the game ends. To feed the fish, press the letter f. To clean the tank, press c. To remove the algae, drag it over the trash can.

Scoring

Scoring is based on the amount of fish and algae left in the tank.

Each fish you save is worth a different amount of points:

  • Molly: 5
  • Nemo: 3
  • Beta: 2

Each algae left in the tank will deduct two from the score.
